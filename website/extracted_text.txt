3.	REPAIRING & PROFILE MATCHING BY EPOXY MORTAR IN THE HRT # 3 AT PLUG BREAKING AREA.
	The Repairing  works of HRT # 3 includes the following :
The repair work was carried out in 23.00 m length of 8.50 m dia of HRT with Epoxy Mortar and Underwater Epoxy Grout.
The filling of HRT # 3 was proposed in the third week of July 2024 and site was handed over on 04.06.2025 The whole work was to be completed in 45 days.
Due to over break during plug breaking, approx 375.00 MT Epoxy Mortar / Underwater Epoxy Grout was consumed. Despite several below given constraints, the work was completed on 10.07.2025 i.e. in 36 Days. The entire material was transported manually in the 700 m length Tunnel in a span of 36 days(shutdown period).
CONSTRAINTS
The repair work carried out at plug breaking area. There was only Intake gate between HPP filled reservoir and working area. The seepage from Intake Gates were coming continuously, which was dewatered through pumps.
As the power failure happened, the pumps operation stopped and water level starts rising. The panic situation creates among the workers, however the power supply restored quickly through DG sets.
The approach to the plug breaking area was only through the Surge Shaft. All material & workers once go from the top of Surge Shaft to Orifice Slab (vertical height 100 m) through Crane & a big Trolley, which was designed for 5 MT load and dia of Surge Shaft (22.00 m). At the orifice slab, all material unloaded and workers also get off from the Trolley because this trolley cannot pass through the orifice. At this stage, some material loaded in another small trolley, which were designed for 1.00 MT load as per the size dia of orifice; and drop down through Crane up to the bottom of the HRT # 3. The plug area of HRT was further 700 m towards Intake Gate. 
The speed of Crane was very less and took approx one hour for one trip from the top of the Surge Shaft to HRT.

RIVER DREDGING & CONSTRUCTION OF BAFFLE WALL ACROSS THE BHAGIRATHI RIVER AT TEHRI PHASE -1
C P Systems was entrusted to execute dredging of Bhagirathi River in the downstream of Tehri Dam spillway (approx. length 640 Rm) by Hindustan Construction Company/THDC, in Feb 2023. It was a very challenging work as the water level used to varies from 602 m to 610.5 m, twice in 24 hrs time period, everyday for a period of 4 months.  At a stretch our team of dedicated managers and engineers used to get shutdown anytime from 11:30 PM to approx. 4:15 AM (the first working period) and between approx. 9:30 AM to 3:30 PM (second working period). These working periods, start time to close time also used to change as per the schedule decided by THDC. The major obstacles faced during the entire project duration were :

RIVER DREDGING-
In variable change of water level (8-10 m twice everyday) continuously during the entire duration of the project.
Mobilizing and de-mobilizing the entire machines like Excavators, Dumpers, Long boom excavator, Backhoe, Hydra, Concrete Boom placers, Concrete Pumps, Dewatering pumps, Shuttering material, welding machines, Micro-piling machines, etc. along with specialized manpower and crew members.
Transportation of the entire dredged material from the river bed to dumping yard at a distance of 14-15 KM.
Whenever the turbine operation used to start, whatever road or dyke constructed at the river bed level, to  make smooth running of the dumpers and machines, used to be washed away, everyday. Everyday there was reconstruction of the dyke and roads to allow smooth operation for the machines thus Project.

CONSTRUCTION OF BAFFLE WALL WITH REINFORCED CEMENT CONCRETE-
The major obstacles faced during the entire project duration were :
In variable change of water level (8-10 m twice everyday) continuously during the entire duration of the project.
Mobilizing and de-mobilizing the entire machines like Long boom excavator, Backhoe, Hydra, Concrete Boom placers, Concrete Pumps, Dewatering pumps, Shuttering material, welding machines, Micro-piling machines, etc. along with specialized manpower and crew members.
There was need of continuous and extensive dewatering required as initially it was decided to dry the river bed level in the areas where concreting for foundation of the baffle wall needed to be done. But even after construction of dyke up to 11 m height from the river bed level, there was continuous water level up to 602 to 604 height from the river bed level, on both side of the dyke.
