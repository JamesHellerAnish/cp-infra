Badrinath Dham

Home / Projects / Badrinath Dham
Heritage Infrastructure

Riverfront Development at Shri Badrinath Dham

A comprehensive infrastructure development project at the holy pilgrimage site of Shri Badrinath Dham along the Alaknanda River, encompassing multiple sub-projects including three riverfronts, a 47m pedestrian bridge, and a civic center building.

Client
THDCIL / Uttarakhand Govt.
Location
Shri Badrinath Dham, Uttarakhand
Bridge Span
47m Pedestrian Bridge
Working Season
April – December
Extreme Conditions

Challenges Faced

🌡️

-10°C to 20°C

Extreme temperature variations. Concrete takes longer to set despite using chemicals. Below zero from Oct–Apr.

🏔️

Himalayan Terrain

Foundations 5-7m below river bed with bouldery strata causing uncontrollable seepage despite 10-12 dewatering pumps.

🛕

Active Yatra Season

May–November yatra restricts equipment movement. Supply of materials hampered. Habitations on both banks interfere with work.

Riverfront A Right Bank of Alaknanda River

Riverfront A — Right Bank Development

Construction of Riverfront A on the right bank of the Alaknanda River at Shri Badrinath Dham. Involved extensive dewatering, foundation excavation in bouldery strata, reinforcement binding, and concrete construction in freezing conditions.

Riverfront F1 Left Bank of Alaknanda River

Riverfront F1 — Left Bank Development

Construction of Riverfront F on the left bank of the Alaknanda River. Similar challenges as Riverfront A with excessive seepage from banks and river side, requiring continuous dewatering operations.

Bridge & Civic Center 47m Span Bridge + Civic Center Building

Pedestrian Bridge & Civic Center Building

Construction of a 47m span pedestrian bridge across the Alaknanda River and a Civic Center Building near Badrish Lake. The bridge foundations are 5-7m below the river bed, making seepage control a major challenge.
